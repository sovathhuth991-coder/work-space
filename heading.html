HTML Heading Levels

Headings provide structure for your page. Use the correct level to show the hierarchy of sections.

Heading 2

h2 is used for major sections underneath the main page title.

Heading 3

h3 works well for subsections inside an h2 section.

Heading 4

This level is good for small groupings or subtopics below h3.

Heading 5

Use h5 for fine-grained subheadings or labels.

Heading 6

h6 is the smallest heading level, usually reserved for the least important section titles.

Tip: Always use headings in order, from h1 to h6, without skipping levels when possible. This improves readability and helps browsers, search engines, and screen readers understand your content.
